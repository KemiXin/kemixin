What is Spring Festival?

Spring festival is widely known as Chinese New Year outside China, but it's also known as Lunar New Year. It's the most important in Chinese festivals, it's part of their culture to celebrate these events. During these event Families reunite, honor their ancestors, share meals, and welcome a good fortune.

According to the legends, that every start of each year a monster named 'Nian' (which means Year), would come and attack the villagers. To drive the monster away, villagers used loud sounds, bright lights and the color red to scare it.

Celebrations often include fireworks, firecrackers, red clothes and decorations as these brings luck and prosperity. That's why young people are given red envelopes.

Key Traditions

  • Red Envelope:Gifts of money symbolizing good luck and blessings.
  • Reunion Dinner:Families gather to share a special meal.
  • Lion and Dragon Dances:It is a performance to chase evil spirits away and bring good fortune.
  • Cleaning the house:It should be done before the New Year to remove old energy and welcome new luck
